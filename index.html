The Model
Dual Strategy — Wholesale Engine + Buy-and-Hold Portfolio

The wholesale operation is not separate from the portfolio — it is the sourcing engine. Every deal evaluated against hold criteria before hitting the disposition list. Deals that qualify get retained. Deals that don’t generate fee income that funds the next acquisition cycle.

Strategy A
Wholesale Acquisition Engine
Deal flow, assignment fees & portfolio sourcing — the operational core
  • Target motivated segments: pre-foreclosure, probate/estate, divorce, absentee, tax delinquency
  • Acquisition at 65-80% of ARV — targeting $30K-$50K+ assignment fee or equity spread
  • 14-21 day contract to close; 30-45 days lead to assignment
  • Capital at risk: $20K-$80K earnest money (refunded at assignment)
  • Disposition: end-buyer list, institutional buyers (Pretium, Amherst, regional funds), own portfolio
  • Revenue: $15K-$60K per assignment; double-close spreads; JV splits
Strategy B
Buy & Hold Portfolio Build
Long-term asset accumulation — the wealth engine for capital partners
  • Retain wholesale-sourced deals meeting criteria: 1%+ rent-to-price; 8%+ cash-on-cash; path-of-growth
  • Tier 1: Columbus, Indianapolis, Kansas City, Cleveland, Cincinnati
  • Assumable mortgage strategy: FHA/VA at 2.5-3.5% vs 6.1% new = ~$900-$1,200/month advantage per door
  • Minimum 5-7 year hold through rate unlock cycle; exit/refi window 2029-2031
  • Target: 50-200 doors across 3-5 markets within 36 months
  • Value-add: light rehab on 40-60% of acquisitions to force equity before tenanting
